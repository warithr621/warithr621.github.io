Allen Robotics

September 2020 - January 2022

  • 2020-2021 FTC Season (12 September 2020 - 22 May 2021):

    Unfortunately since Slack does not allows scrolling over ten thousand messages due to subscription, I am at the moment unable to collect any exact data on placings that we had. However, I can definitely say that my first year participating in a Robotics club was a wonderful experience. Part of the programming team on FTC Team 12106 at Lowery Freshman Center, we met twice a week in order to discuss the many aspects going into this competition: monetary situations, building the robot, programming its various functions, and presenting our progress at competitions throughout the year. We also created a YouTube channel that displays the progress from both of our school's teams: 12106 and 15084. An example of one of these videos is embeded on your right.

  • 2021-2022 BEST Season (September 2021 - December 2022):

    This was my first year as part of the AHS Robotics team, and I really enjoyed meeting so many new people both in my grade and in the Classes of 2022 and 2023. Alongside several others, I worked on programming our robot for the BEST competition. From the local Collin County BEST competition we advanced to the state competition, where we placed 2nd out of all competing teams.

High School UIL

November 2020 - Present

  • 2021 UIL Season:

    This was my first year competing in UIL at the high school level. Although just a freshman, I was able to make my way into six different UIL teams. I managed to advance in all six of these--either individually or through team--to the regional competition. Out of these six I only made state in two of these competitions, but I was also part of the UIL Spelling Team that won 1st at the state competition :D

  • 2022 UIL Season:

    With Year 2 underway, and my first one in-person due to COVID the past year, I was really excited to see what was to come. For districts I once again made six different teams, although this year I made it in for science rather than spelling. I managed to qualify for regionals in all six competitions I did, including placing top 3 individually at five of these competitions at districts. I then managed to qualify in five competitions to state, with the exception of Computer Applications. This also included being a part of the first Allen Computer Science team to make UIL State, and being the first calculator state qualifier from Allen in five years. Although I wasn't able to place individually in any of my competitions, I was a part of the 3rd place state team in three categories: Number Sense, Science, and General Math.

Competitive Programming

08 January 2021 - Present

  • USACO:

    USACO is the primary programming contest in the United States, hosting four competitions annually and allowing the highest-ranked students to participate in a summer camp leading to IOI, the International Olympiad in Informatics.

    I started competing in USACO at the start of my freshman year, score a 513 on the 2021 January Contest. After about a year's worth of preparation, I scored high enough on the December Contest later that year in order to promote to the Silver division where I currently am.

  • Codeforces:

    I started getting into competitve programming around New Year's Day 2021, and this is majorly because of UIL CS' Programming Section as well as friends that I have met recently. Codeforces is the first non-USACO website that I have used to practice and compete in coding competitions. On this website I primarily submit solutions in C++ (though I often switch to Java, especially in prep for UIL), and I'm currently rated as a pupil as of May 25 2022.

  • GitHub:

    Obviously, GitHub is not a source for active programming contests. However, I've started to upload any submissions I have made on my Comp-Programming repository, which includes submissions from CodeForces, AtCoder, USACO, and any other programming contests I have participated in.
